# Protein-Glycan Docking Benchmarks

This directory contains benchmarking scenarios for protein-glycan docking using HADDOCK3. Glycans are carbohydrate chains attached to proteins and lipids, and they play critical roles in cell signalling, immune recognition, and pathogen-host interactions.

These benchmarks use topological interaction (TI) restraints derived from the glycan structure to guide docking, and evaluate multiple clustering strategies to identify the best-performing protocol for protein-glycan complexes.

## Dataset

- **Input list**: `protein-glycan-input.txt` — generated by running `setup.sh`
- **Molecule suffixes**: `_r_b` (receptor, bound), `_l_b` (glycan, bound), `_r_u` (receptor, unbound), `_l_u` (glycan, unbound), `_l_ensemble` (glycan conformational ensemble for `scenario_unbound_ens_vdw_tip-ap`)
- **Restraint files**: `_ti-aa.tbl` (true interface on both protein and glycan), `_tip-ap.tbl` (true interface on protein, glycan fully passive)
- **Reference structure**: `_analysis.pdb` — used for CAPRI evaluation

## About Ambiguous Interaction Restraints

Ambiguous Interaction Restraints (AIRs) guide docking by defining potential contacts between the protein and glycan. The benchmark includes two AIR schemes:

- **`ti-aa`**: true interface residues are defined on both the protein and glycan.
- **`tip-ap`**: true interface residues are defined on the protein, while the glycan is treated as fully passive.

## Scenarios

### scenario_bound_vdw_ti-aa

The simplest scenario: both the protein receptor and the glycan ligand are provided in their bound (co-crystal) conformations. TI restraints derived from the all-atom glycan structure are used alongside a van der Waals energy term (`w_vdw: 1`) during rigid-body docking.

### scenario_unbound_vdw_tip-ap

The protein and glycan are both provided in their unbound conformations, making this a more realistic and challenging scenario. The restraints are switched from `ti-aa` to `tip-ap` (true interface protein, all passive), where active residues are defined on the protein only and the glycan is treated as fully passive.

### scenario_unbound_ens_vdw_tip-ap

An extended version of `scenario_unbound_vdw_tip-ap` that incorporates ensemble-based sampling. Multiple conformations of the unbound glycan (provided as a structural ensemble) are used during the rigid-body stage to better represent the conformational space accessible to the free glycan.
